HDI Computation Methodology

Understanding the mathematical components behind the Human Development Index.

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a summary measure of average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, being knowledgeable, and having a decent standard of living. The index is computed as the geometric mean of normalized indices for each of the three dimensions:

1. Long & Healthy Life

Measured by Life Expectancy at Birth. The health dimension is normalized using a minimum value of 20 years and a maximum value of 85 years.

2. Access to Knowledge

Measured by combining Expected Years of Schooling (capped at 18) and Mean Years of Schooling (capped at 15).

3. Decent Standard of Living

Measured by Gross National Income (GNI) Per Capita (PPP $). Uses log scaling to represent diminishing returns of income utility (capped at $75,000).

Mathematical Formulation

Step 1: Normalize Dimension Indices

\(I_{Health} = \frac{\text{Life Expectancy} - 20}{85 - 20}\)

\(I_{Education} = \frac{I_{ExpectedSchooling} + I_{MeanSchooling}}{2} = \frac{\frac{\text{EYS}}{18} + \frac{\text{MYS}}{15}}{2}\)

\(I_{Income} = \frac{\ln(\text{GNIpc}) - \ln(100)}{\ln(75000) - \ln(100)}\)

Step 2: Compute Geometric Mean

\(HDI = \left( I_{Health} \cdot I_{Education} \cdot I_{Income} \right)^{1/3}\)
